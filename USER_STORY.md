# User Story

Maya is building a small product feature under hackathon pressure. She asks Codex to create the experience, watches the change appear, and clicks through the obvious path. It looks good enough at first glance.

But she does not feel ready to ship. The code was generated quickly. The happy path works. The uncertainty is in everything she did not think to check.

She opens Verion.

There is no setup ritual and no long checklist. The product gives her one clear action: Verify. She presses it.

Verion begins exploring the application like a careful reviewer. It moves through the product, tries realistic interactions, and watches for behavior that would matter to a user. Maya does not need to write a test plan or guess where the weak spot might be.

Verion finds an issue. Instead of presenting a wall of failures, it groups the symptoms into one likely root cause. The explanation is short enough to understand immediately and specific enough to act on.

Maya sends the prepared context to Codex. Codex fixes the issue with the focused information Verion provided.

Then Maya runs Verion again.

This time, the broken path holds. The original issue no longer appears. Verion shows that the fix changed the outcome, not just the code.

Maya ships the feature with a different feeling than she had ten minutes earlier. Not because the product promised perfection, but because the important path was checked, the issue was explained, the fix was verified, and the remaining decision felt clear.
